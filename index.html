Guangxing Paper Mill:
UX/UI Digital Redesign

language View Redesigned Demo Site arrow_forward
Role Product Owner
Core Tools
Figma/Webnode
Background & Constraints

The "About Us" section of the Guangxing Paper Mill website faced severe UX issues, including chaotic information architecture, inconsistent visual styles, and broken interactions. The goal was to reorganize the content to create a digital storefront that reflects its traditional cultural heritage while delivering a modern user experience.

Phase 1: Pain-point & IA Restructuring

Before starting the design, I conducted an in-depth "phenomenon organization and problem reproduction" of the legacy site.

  • Pain-point Mind Mapping: Identified 3 core issues: fragmented information causing navigation difficulties, lack of variation in sub-pages leading to reading fatigue, and a cumbersome language switching process.

  • IA Convergence: Applied Information Architecture principles to consolidate the scattered "7 pages, 6 menus" into a logically clear "5 pages, 4 menus" structure, significantly reducing users' cognitive load.

Phase 2: Design System & User Experience

Starting from user needs, I balanced "traditional cultural heritage" with a "modern digital experience."

  • Cross-language Design System: Established a unified UI component library, a primary color palette (White / Light Gray / Dark Blue), and typography guidelines (Microsoft JhengHei + Times New Roman) to resolve visual fragmentation.

  • Intuitive Interaction: Referenced modern web layouts to transform previously click-to-jump content into a seamless single-page scroll. Additionally, placed an intuitive language switch button at the top for international visitors.

Phase 3: Collaboration & Learnings

Focusing on cross-team communication, proposing initial hypotheses, and executing standard deliveries.

  • Standardized Design Delivery: Deeply understood the pain points of design-to-development communication. By creating Figma files with comprehensive instructions and usage guidelines, I effectively reduced back-and-forth confirmations during development.

  • Uncovering Behavioral Logic: Learned to go beyond superficial "ugly layout" problems and dig deeper into *why* users couldn't find information, ensuring every page had a clear functional purpose.

Data Product Manager Python & Streamlit

Spotify Intelligence:
From Data Insights to Business Decision Dashboard

insights View Live Dashboard arrow_forward
Role Data Product Manager
Core Tools
Python/Plotly/Streamlit
Background

Developed independently at TU Berlin. This data product transforms massive raw music datasets into an actionable "Business Decision Dashboard" for music producers and marketing teams, driving decisions from "Descriptive" to "Prescriptive."

Phase 1: Pain-point Convergence & Data Wrangling

In the early development stages, I identified three core business pain points for music producers and A&R teams: discovering low-cost high-potential artists, predicting audience taste trends, and assessing production risks.

  • Data Cleaning & Feature Extraction: Faced with 15 years of noisy raw Spotify data, I rigorously filtered out invalid genres and applied logical conditions to transform continuous numerical values (e.g., Tempo) into business-meaningful categorical tags (e.g., Slow, Mainstream), establishing a clean data foundation for subsequent decision-making.

▲ Dashboard & Feature Extraction Logic in Python

Phase 2: Core Product Features & Logic

To address the identified pain points, I combined business acumen with data engineering to design three core feature modules:

track_changes

Dark Horse Radar

Designed a four-quadrant algorithm to filter micro-influencers with "low follower counts but extreme popularity," helping record labels maximize ROI.

radar

Hit Potential Predictor

Built a simulator allowing producers to adjust demo track audio features via interactive sliders, instantly generating a "Predicted Hit Score".

pie_chart

Format Strategy Performance

Utilized side-by-side visual analysis to illustrate the market performance differences between "Singles" and "Albums" across various genres.

Phase 3: Interactive UI & Cloud Deployment

A data product must transcend local environments; it requires a seamless user experience to be truly adopted and implemented.

  • Interactive Storytelling: Abandoned static charts in favor of Plotly to create dynamic bubble charts and global heatmaps. Users can visually track how audience tastes shift towards "high energy and high danceability" along a timeline.

  • Full-Stack Deployment: Encapsulated Python logic using Streamlit to design a modern Web App with sidebars and multiple tabs. Successfully deployed it to the cloud via Git version control, completing end-to-end product delivery.

🏆 Reflections & PM Mindset Growth

This high-intensity full-stack development journey profoundly cemented my understanding of a "Data Product Manager's" core value: Great analytics shouldn't just be flashy charts; they must clearly answer business questions, provide actionable options, and ultimately drive valuable decisions. This data-driven mindset will be my strongest asset for future product iteration and optimization.
